A primer on Haystaq scores, how to present sentiment data, and the constituent quote.

Reference: https://haystaqdna.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/L2-National-Models-User-Guide-2024-Updated-w-Com.pdf

## What Haystaq scores are

Modeled voter attitudes on a 0-100 scale derived from L2 voter file data. These are not survey results -- they are modeled estimates. A score of 72 means 72% of voters in the segment are modeled to hold that position.

## Sentiment format

Bold the top-line numbers. Follow with one sentence of district-level specificity if available.

Example:
**72% support · 18% oppose**
Northside support climbs to 81%. Camera-request volume from the Ramsey corridor is 3.4x the citywide average over the last 12 months.

When no data is available:
No sentiment data yet for [item name].

## What to say / what not to say

Say: "residents in this district are estimated to...", "modeled support stands at..."
Do not say: "X% of voters support" (implies a direct survey), "data shows voters believe" (overstates certainty)

## Constituent quote

A verbatim quote from a real constituent that humanizes the data. Present in a blockquote with the constituent's name.

If no quote is available: *No constituent quote captured yet.*

The quote should reflect lived experience behind the numbers -- not restate the statistic.
