### Item 6C — Ordinance 26-D: Commercial Vehicle and Oversized Vehicle Parking
**Amend Chapter 24, Traffic and Vehicles | Requires vote**

#### Overview

Council is being asked to adopt Ordinance 26-D, which makes two significant changes to Chapter 24 of the Code of Ordinances.

First, it converts the existing blanket ban on parking vehicles over 25 feet in length from an all-day prohibition to an overnight-only restriction (10 p.m. to 6 a.m.). This resolves an unintended consequence in the current ordinance that technically prohibited landscaping companies and similar service businesses from parking service vehicle-trailer combinations on city streets during daytime hours.

Second, it adds a new section (Sec. 24-49) restricting commercial motor vehicles — defined to include road tractors, truck tractors, pole trailers, and semitrailers — from parking on city streets except for immediate loading or unloading during daytime hours, and prohibiting all such parking between 10 p.m. and 6 a.m.

Violations carry a penalty not to exceed $500 per day; each day is a separate offense. The ordinance takes effect immediately upon passage, with the penalty provision taking effect ten days after publication. This item was developed in response to resident complaints and a council workshop held January 15, 2026.

### Item 6D — Resolution 26-R-20: MVCPA Grant Application for Drone First Responder System and FLOCK ALPR Cameras
**Authorize APD grant application | Requires vote | Grant deadline May 8, 2026**

#### Overview

Council is being asked to adopt Resolution 26-R-20, authorizing the Alvin Police Department to apply for a Fiscal Year 2027 Catalytic Converter Theft Prevention Grant through the Motor Vehicle Crime Prevention Authority (MVCPA). The grant application deadline is May 8, 2026 — three weeks from this meeting. A council resolution is required by MVCPA to submit the application.

If awarded, the grant would fund two items: (1) eight additional FLOCK ALPR license plate reader cameras for the FM 1462/SH 288 corridor, and (2) a Drone First Responder (DFR) program in which a drone is automatically dispatched to calls in progress and can arrive on scene within 90 seconds, ahead of responding officers. The DFR system would integrate with existing Flock ALPR cameras and the city's dispatch system.

The grant program reimburses 80% of eligible costs quarterly; the city is required to fund costs upfront and commit to a 20% cash match. The anticipated city cost for the first grant year is approximately $64,000.00. Without the grant, the annual cost would range between $150,000 and $310,000 depending on the DFR tier chosen. Adopting this resolution authorizes the application only — it does not commit the city to accept an award or appropriate any funds.
