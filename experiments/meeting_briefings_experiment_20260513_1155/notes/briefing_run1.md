### Parking Ordinance Amendment - Regulating Large and Commercial Vehicle Parking

**Overview**
The City Council will consider Ordinance 26-D, which amends Chapter 24 to regulate parking of oversized and commercial vehicles on city streets. The ordinance addresses complaints received by Council and police about oversize and commercial vehicle parking. Following a January 15, 2026 workshop requested by Council Member DeKeyzer, staff identified technical issues with the existing ordinance. The new ordinance prohibits overnight parking (10 PM - 6 AM) of vehicles exceeding 25 feet and all commercial motor vehicles. During daytime hours, commercial vehicles may park only for immediate loading or unloading. Violations carry penalties up to $500. Permitted construction vehicles within construction zones are exempt.

**Key Observations**
- Ordinance originated from multiple complaints received by Council and police department
- Council Member DeKeyzer requested the January 15, 2026 workshop that led to this proposal
- Current ordinance creates unintended violations for lawn service companies pulling equipment trailers
- New language allows daytime parking for commercial vehicles conducting immediate loading/unloading
- Enforcement responsibility falls to Alvin Police Department

### Catalytic Converter Theft Prevention Grant - Drone First Responder and FLOCK Cameras

**Overview**
The City Council will consider Resolution 26-R-20, authorizing the Alvin Police Department to apply for the Texas Motor Vehicle Crime Prevention Authority grant for the period September 1, 2026 to August 31, 2027. The grant provides 80% reimbursement (20% city match) for technology to combat motor vehicle theft and related crimes. The application requests funding for a Drone First Responder (DFR) system and eight additional FLOCK ALPR cameras to be deployed in the FM 1462/SH 288 area in response to rapid development. The DFR program enables drone response to calls in progress, typically arriving overhead within 90 seconds with 4-mile radius coverage. Officers can view live feeds in-car before arrival, increasing safety and effectiveness.

**Constituent Sentiment**
Support: 56.5% | Oppose: 40.3%
GoodParty.org's modeled data estimates that approximately 56.5% of active voters in Alvin demonstrate higher levels of trust in police, while 40.3% score below the moderate threshold on this measure. Regarding violent crime concerns, 49.6% of constituents are estimated to be very worried about violent crime. These estimates reflect modeled constituent attitudes and do not represent direct survey responses.
