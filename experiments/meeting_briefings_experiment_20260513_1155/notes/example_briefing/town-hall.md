## Public Safety Camera Expansion

### Overview

You're voting on the vendor contract and camera locations across the city. The vendor decision is mostly staff's call. The location map is where your voice matters. Look at the map before Monday. If the northside and Ramsey Street corridor aren't on it, say so before the vote. Your silence will be read as agreement.

### Constituent Sentiment

**72% support · 18% oppose**

Northside support climbs to 81%. Camera-request volume from the Ramsey corridor is 3.4x the citywide average over the last 12 months.

### Constituent Quote

> "I walk my kids to the bus stop on Ramsey before sunrise. I'm not asking for a police state. I'm asking for a camera on the corner." — Mike Rodriguez

### Budget Impact

$1.2M one-time install + $180K/yr ops. Spread across the levy, that's ~$8.40/household one-time and ~$1.30/household/yr ongoing.

### Talking Points

- 72% citywide support and 81% on the Northside. The location map should reflect where the demand actually is.
- Councilmember Brennan voted yes on the 2024 downtown camera package on a 65% support read; this proposal has higher support and 3.4x the request volume in the underserved blocks.
- Ask Councilmember Pak to attach the same quarterly privacy and access audit clause she pushed for on the 2024 body-cam contract.

## FY2027 Property Tax Rate Direction

### Overview

There's no vote Monday, but this crucial conversation sets the table before numbers are locked in. What you say now shapes what the City Manager puts in front of you in May. Your community wants lower taxes and better services. Don't commit to a plan yet. Walk in with a clear point of view. Focus on values for now — that's what keeps your options open and your credibility intact.

### Constituent Sentiment

No sentiment data yet for FY2027 Property Tax Rate Direction.

### Constituent Quote

> "I'm on a fixed income. Another twenty-eight dollars a year doesn't sound like much to you, but it's my prescription co-pay for two months." — Eleanor Voss

### Budget Impact

Each 1¢ above flat = ~$310K in annual revenue, or ~$28/yr on a $200K home. Stacked with the proposed stormwater fee bump, the combined household impact is closer to $60/yr.

### Talking Points

- 54% oppose any increase, up 9 points since March. Staff need to bring projects to the table, not just a rate.
- Mayor Decker pledged "no increase without a project list" during the FY26 cycle. Quote it back to him and ask for the list before the May 21 first reading.
- $28/yr per household sounds small until you stack it on the proposed stormwater fee. Together that's ~$60/yr, and 71% of homeowners 65+ already oppose any increase.

## Ramsey Street Corridor Revitalization

### Overview

There is no vote on this item, but there is a decision: let it stay a planning conversation, or push for one real, public commitment before the meeting ends. Before Monday, call or text one business owner or resident on Ramsey Street. Ask what meaningful progress looks like to them. Then, bring that voice into the room. It's more powerful than any prepared talking point.

### Constituent Sentiment

**61% support · 12% oppose**

61% want visible progress this fiscal year, 12% oppose. Planning fatigue is high: 73% of corridor business owners surveyed say they need a deliverable schedule before they'll attend another workshop.

### Constituent Quote

*No constituent quote captured yet.*

### Budget Impact

$4M TIF contribution amortized over 10 years ≈ $11/household over the life of the increment if absorbed by the levy. The downtown banner program, by comparison, ran $22/household over 10 years.

### Talking Points

- 61% of constituents want visible progress this fiscal year. A one-page deliverable schedule from staff is the floor, not the ask.
- Councilmember Liu sponsored the 2023 corridor study. Ask her on the record to co-sponsor a deliverable motion tonight. She has cover with 73% of corridor business owners behind it.
- $11/household over 10 years is half what we spent on the downtown banner program. Frame the cost that way before opponents anchor it as "another $4M."
