### Budget Impact

- $28/yr per household sounds small until you stack it on the proposed stormwater fee. Together that's ~$60/yr, and 71% of homeowners 65+ already oppose any increase.

# Full Briefing: Ramsey Street Corridor Revitalization

## Agenda Item

**Ramsey Street Corridor Revitalization**

**Read aloud**

Final ratification of the conditions package for the $24M mixed-use development on the former mill site, including a $4M city contribution via TIF. The framework has been negotiated for months and tonight closes the loop.

## Decisions You Are Being Asked To Make

You're voting on the final conditions package, including the affordable housing set-aside, compliance triggers, and public reporting cadence. Approving without enforceable conditions makes them aspirational rather than binding.

## Why It Matters

Smart growth on the Ramsey Street corridor is a top governance priority. Sentiment sits at 71.3 / 100 and the Northside Neighborhood Association endorsed unanimously. The 15% affordable set-aside, up from the original 12%, is a direct result of community input.

## Talking Points For The Meeting

- 15% affordable set-aside, up from the original 12%, a direct result of constituent input. Call that out: this is what showing up gets you.
- Northside Neighborhood Association endorsed unanimously and sentiment sits at 71.3/100. A yes vote delivers a visible win on a stated campaign priority.
- Keep your yes contingent on enforceable compliance triggers and public reporting cadence. Without them, the 15% set-aside is aspirational, not binding. Flag any last-minute language changes during member discussion.

## Action Item

Confirm the final conditions document still reflects the 15% set-aside before you cast your vote, and flag any last-minute language changes during member discussion.
