## Ordinance 26-D — Commercial Vehicle and Oversized Vehicle Parking Restrictions
*Item 6C · vote*

You're voting on a new parking ordinance that restricts large vehicles and commercial motor vehicles on city streets. The core change: vehicles over 25 feet can no longer park on city streets between 10 PM and 6 AM, and commercial motor vehicles are banned from street parking at all times except for active loading and unloading. A construction zone exemption is included. The penalty is up to $500 per violation per day. This came out of a January 15, 2026 workshop requested by Council Member DeKeyzer after resident complaints about oversized and commercial vehicles parked in neighborhoods.

**Decision required:** Approve Ordinance 26-D as written. No budget impact.

**Why it matters:** The current ordinance had an unintended consequence: lawn care companies with truck-and-trailer combos exceeding 25 feet were technically in violation for any daytime street parking. The new language fixes that by restricting the 25-foot rule to overnight hours only, while separately banning commercial motor vehicles around the clock (except during active loading). The result is cleaner neighborhoods without penalizing service workers.

**Budget impact:** No direct expenditure. Enforcement costs are absorbed by APD. Fine revenue is incidental. No budget amendment required.

**Presenting:** Robert E. Lee, Police Chief
